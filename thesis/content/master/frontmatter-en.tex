\thesisabstract{%
Multimodal cyber-physical systems can improve situational awareness, but their predictions may become unreliable when one sensor is noisy, delayed, or absent. This master thesis proposes a reliability-aware fusion architecture and a reproducible protocol that evaluate accuracy, calibration, robustness, latency, and resource use under controlled modality loss. In the illustrative results, the proposed method reaches 85.1\% macro F1 with 30\% missing modalities, compared with 69.0\% for the baseline, while maintaining a median latency of 27~ms; these numbers are placeholders. The contribution is a claim-driven methodology that links each design choice to explicit evidence, limitations, and conditions of validity.
}
